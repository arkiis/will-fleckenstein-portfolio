TWINE

A student-to-student marketplace for campus services.

A mobile product concept that helps UF students discover, trust, and book student-run services in one place.

Role
Product Designer

Focus
Research · UX/UI · Brand · Interaction

Platform
iOS Mobile App

Year
2026

The Challenge
How might we make student services easier to find, trust, and manage for both consumers and entrepreneurs?
Problem & Opportunity
PROBLEM & OPPORTUNITY

Student services are valuable, but hard to discover, trust, and manage.

1. Low visibility of student services
Many students do not know these services exist.

2. Fragmented communication
Entrepreneurs juggle Instagram, forms, and messaging apps.

3. Trust gaps
Consumers rely on word-of-mouth and cannot easily verify quality.

4. Inefficient discovery
No centralized place to browse or compare services.

5. Operational challenges
Entrepreneurs lack simple tools and performance insights.

Opportunity
Twine brings discovery, trust, communication, and growth tools into one campus-focused marketplace.
Research Approach
RESEARCH APPROACH

Semi-structured interviews with both sides of the marketplace.

Method
Semi-structured interviews

Participants
4 student entrepreneurs
3 student consumers

Why this method
Balance between consistency and flexibility
Allows deeper follow-up and richer insights

Structure
10 core questions per participant
Role-specific questions for each group

Goal
Understand how services are marketed, discovered, and evaluated — then identify how a centralized platform could support both groups.

What We Learned
WHAT WE LEARNED

Consumers, entrepreneurs, and the broader system all pointed to the same structural gaps.

Consumers

Trust is critical
Reviews, portfolios, and recommendations matter most.

Top decision factors
Cost
Quality
Turnaround time

Convenience matters
Fast responses and easy booking are expected.

Entrepreneurs

Visibility is the biggest challenge
Many rely heavily on Instagram and word-of-mouth.

Operations feel fragmented
Communication is scattered across multiple tools.

Entrepreneurs want structure
They need centralized client management and built-in analytics.

Shared System Insight

Service discovery is broken
There is no single place to find student services.

Trust drives decisions
Social proof, speed, and reliability matter.

Both groups want more structure
Consumers want easier browsing and clearer trust signals.
Entrepreneurs want better organization and tools.

Takeaway
The opportunity is not just a marketplace — it is a clearer system for discovery, trust, and growth.

Design Principles
DESIGN PRINCIPLES

A small set of principles guided the interface.

1. Visual Hierarchy
Clear structure, alignment for scanning, and progressive disclosure help students move through the product with less effort.

2. Contextual Guidance
The interface supports users with guidance tailored to their current task.

3. Simple & Recognizable Design
Simple, universal icons make actions easier to recognize and understand.

4. Anthropomorphism
The gator mascot helps the system feel more social, friendly, and approachable.

5. Persuading Through Praise
Positive feedback can motivate entrepreneurs to stay engaged and improve.

6. Feedback
Integrated feedback patterns help users understand system states and changes consistently.

Create a polished UX case study for Twine, a student-to-student marketplace app for UF students.

Twine helps student consumers discover, trust, and book student-run services while helping student entrepreneurs manage visibility, communication, credibility, and growth.

The problem is that student services are scattered across Instagram, group chats, flyers, word-of-mouth, and individual forms. Consumers struggle to discover and verify services, while entrepreneurs struggle to manage communication, build trust, and track growth.

The research used semi-structured interviews with 4 student entrepreneurs and 3 student consumers. The goal was to understand how services are marketed, discovered, evaluated, and managed.

Key findings:
Consumers care most about trust, quality, cost, turnaround time, and convenience.
Entrepreneurs struggle with visibility, scattered communication, and lack of operational tools.
Both groups need a more structured system for discovery, trust, booking, messaging, and growth.

The solution is Twine: a campus-focused marketplace with discovery cards, ratings, reviews, service profiles, messaging, starter tips, favorites, and entrepreneur insights.

The visual identity uses a knot symbol to represent connection, UF-inspired blue for trust and campus relevance, and a friendly gator mascot to make the product feel approachable.

Design principles:
Visual hierarchy
Contextual guidance
Simple and recognizable icons
Anthropomorphism
Persuading through praise
Consistent feedback

The final product evolved from low-fidelity flows into a high-fidelity mobile experience focused on discovery, trust, messaging, and creator growth.
